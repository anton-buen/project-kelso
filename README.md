# KELSO: Learning Drums Without Drums

Welcome to Project-Kelso. This is a personal passion project. 

How do you learn to play the drums when you don't actually own a drum set? Because of noise limits, tiny apartments, and expensive gear, practicing drums at home is often impossible. This app was built to bypass all of that. It lets you practice the real, high-level physical and mental coordination of drumming using nothing but your hands and a flat desk.

In particular, this project focuses on leveling up your weak hand (non-dominant hand). When learning drums, your weak hand naturally lags behind, rushes when you get tired, and tenses up immediately. This app acts as a digital personal trainer to help your weak hand match your strong hand's relaxed, perfect rhythm.

---

## What is this?

Usually, practicing "drums" on a desk is a guessing game. You tap your fingers, but you do not know if your timing is actually perfect, or if you are tensing up your body to go faster.

Project-Kelso turns your computer into a **smart practice mirror**:

1. **The Mic hears your taps** and measures exactly how many milliseconds early or late you are compared to the metronome.
2. **The Camera watches your shoulders** to see if you are tensing up.
3. **An AI Coach** reads all this data and gives you a simple, personalized report on how to improve.

## The Science Behind It

This project is inspired by real scientific studies on human movement and learning (neuro-motor training).

When musicians try to play faster or get better, they often make two mistakes:

* **Micro-timing errors:** Your brain thinks you are perfectly on beat, but you might actually be rushing by 50 milliseconds. You cannot easily hear this, but your body "feels" it.
* **Invisible tension:** To play fast, people naturally tighten their shoulders. This actually slows you down, ruins your rhythm, and can cause painful hand injuries like tendonitis.

Why is it named "Kelso"?
This project is named after Dr. J.A. Scott Kelso, a world-famous neuroscientist and pioneer of Coordination Dynamics (the study of how the brain, nervous system, and muscles coordinate movement).

>Dr. Kelso is famous for his breakthrough finger-tapping experiments (known scientifically as the HKB Model). He discovered that when humans tap their hands at a slow speed, they can easily maintain complex, relaxed rhythms. But as the metronome speeds up past a certain point, the brain undergoes a "phase transition"—the muscles suddenly lock up, coordinate incorrectly, and slip into forced, tense synchronization.
>
>Project-Kelso is a direct application of his research. By measuring your tap-timing down to the millisecond while simultaneously watching your shoulders for tension, I'm using Dr. Kelso's exact coordination models to map out exactly when your nervous system begins to panic and tense up under speed.

### Solution: Biofeedback

Studies show that the human brain learns incredibly fast when it gets **immediate, objective feedback**.

Instead of guessing, Kelso tells you the cold truth:

> *“You are hitting 100 milliseconds too fast, and your left shoulder is tensing up by 30%.”*

By seeing this instantly (the screen glows red when you tense), your brain quickly learns to connect *feeling relaxed* with *playing on time*. This builds "good muscle memory" from day one.

## How to Use It

I designed this to keep you in a distraction-free "flow state."

1. **Sit in Frame:** Sit back so the camera can clearly see both of your shoulders.
2. **Pick a Hand:** Press **L** to train your left hand, or **R** to train your right hand.
3. **The Silent Tare (2 Seconds):** Sit perfectly still and relax. For 2 seconds, the camera measures your resting posture.
4. **Practice:** Tap your desk to the metronome. If you tense up, the screen glows red to warn you.
5. **Get Results:** Press **Enter** to stop. An AI will read your session and write a custom diagnostic report. Press **Tab** anytime to view your history.
